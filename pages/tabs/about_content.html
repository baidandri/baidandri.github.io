About WHO


Windmill Hill Observatory houses a Meade 0.4 m LX200GPS telescope. The Telescope features a 0.4 m diameter primary mirror with a 4064mm focal length and f/10 focal ratio. The Telescope itself weights approximately 100kg. For many years this Telescope was situated at the University of Warwick's La Palma Observatory carrying out astronomical research. It was relocated to the Windmill Hill Observatory in 2022.

FLI ProLine 4710 CCD camera


The camera is an FLI ProLine 4710 CCD camera, with a red-sensitive deep depleted sensor that is the camera is more sensitive to the red wavelengths (higher achievable Quantum Efficiency, QE). It has a back-illuminated architecture with 1056 x 1027 active pixels. The maximum capacity per pixel (also known as full well capacity or FWC) is 100k electrons before saturation occurs. It operates in three speed modes (750 kHz, 2 MHz, 4 MHz) with an average readout noise of 9 electrons. The cooling performance is reaching -45 ° C and reduces the dark current to 0.05 electrons/pixel/sec.
